# Product Walkthrough — The Human Story (Non-Technical)

Read this like you got hired yesterday and don't know a button from a banana. No tech talk.
This is the ATS explained as a *journey*, front door to back door.

**What this tool actually is:** a **hunting-and-hiring tracker**. The company finds healthcare
workers (nurses, doctors, therapists) and drops them into US clinics that need staff. Your whole
job lives inside this thing.

---

## 🚪 The front door — logging in
- App opens, asks *"who are you?"* Type email + password, or hit the Google button. That's
  **logging in** — telling the system "it's me, let me in."
- The system knows your name and your rank (owner, manager, screener, associate). Your rank
  decides which rooms are open and which are locked. A regular guy sees fewer doors than the boss.

## 🏠 You walk in — the Overview (home)
- Your **morning desk**. "Good morning, here's what's on fire today."
- Shows your daily targets, a short AI note ("do this first"), a to-do list of urgent people, and
  what changed since you last left. Read it, know where to start. Done.

---

## The actual work — where do people come from?
A "candidate" (a nurse, a doctor) doesn't appear by magic. They have a journey. Follow the person.

### 1. Finding them → Sourcing & Discover
- You need fresh people. Two ways to hunt:
  - **Discover** — search a big government doctor phonebook (NPPES). "Give me all the nurses in
    Florida." It spits out names. Grab the good ones.
  - **Sourcing** — your **cold-contact list**: people you found but haven't hired yet. You message
    them ("hey, interested in a job?"). That's **outreach**. You log each try: 1st message, 2nd
    message, no reply, replied-hot, replied-cold. It's a chase.
- Someone's interested? You **promote** them. "Promote" just means: *move this person out of the
  maybe-pile into the real hiring line.*

### 2. Bringing them in → Parse Resume / Add Candidate
- The person sends a resume (a PDF). Drop it into **Parse Resume**. The AI reads the PDF and
  auto-fills their info — no typing. Now they're a real **candidate** in the system.

### 3. The hiring line → Pipeline (the heart)
- The big board. Every candidate is a **card** that moves left-to-right through stages:
  *New → Screened → Reviewed → Sent to client → Interview → Offer → Started day one.*
- Drag their card forward as things progress. The system blocks you from skipping steps (can't
  send a nurse to a clinic if her license isn't verified yet). That's the **pipeline** — the
  assembly line of a human getting hired.

### 4. Checking them → Screening & Verify
- Before sending someone to a clinic, grade them: **Screening** gives a score (do they fit? right
  license? right pay?). And you **verify** their medical license is real and active.
  Green light or red light.

### 5. Talking to them → Templates
- You send a lot of emails. **Templates** are pre-written messages with blanks the system
  auto-fills (their name, the clinic). Pick one, hit send, it logs it. No writing from scratch.

### 6. The full folder → Candidate Detail
- Click any card and a **full folder** opens on that person: info, license, resume, and a notes
  wall where teammates chat and @-tag each other. This is where you actually *work* one person.

### 7. A reply lands → Inbound Triage
- Someone messages you back ("yeah, I'm interested"). Paste that message into **Inbound Triage** —
  the AI reads it, pulls out who they are, and turns them into a **hot lead** already matched to a
  role. One paste instead of typing it all in. Keeps you fast when replies are flying in.

---

## The other side — the clients (the clinics paying you)

### 8. CRM — managing the clinics
- The clinics that hire your people are **clients**. CRM is your **relationship room**: who's the
  contact, are they happy or about to leave (churn risk), how much money they bring, deals in
  progress. Basically "keep the customer sweet."

### 9. Open Roles — the shopping list
- Each clinic says "I need 3 nurses in Texas." Those are **open roles** — the orders you're trying
  to fill. The system even suggests which of your candidates match each order.

### 10. Client Portal — the window you let them peek through
- A **read-only view** you share with a clinic so they can see the candidates you lined up for
  them. They look, they don't touch.

---

## The scoreboard — how's everyone doing?

### 11. Daily Brief & Weekly Brief
- End-of-day / end-of-week summaries. "Here's what the team did, what's stuck, the plan."
  Auto-written by AI, you tweak and send it out.

### 12. Reports & Perf
- The **stats room**. Charts and numbers: how fast people get hired, who's the top recruiter,
  which clinics are winning. For the bosses.

### 13. Daily Log & Journal
- Your **personal diary**. "Today I sourced 20, sent 15 messages." Track your own grind and hit
  your targets.

---

## The back office

### 14. Bulk Import
- You've got an old spreadsheet with 2,000 people from before this tool existed. **Import** =
  shove that whole file in at once instead of typing them one by one. The system matches resumes
  to names and loads them all up. (That's what "import" means — *mass-loading old data in one
  shot.*)

### 15. Admin
- The **control panel** (bosses only): add teammates, set who sees what, block people, reset
  passwords.

### 16. Learn
- A little **tutorial corner** — lessons that teach you the app.

---

## 🚪 The back door — logging out
- Click sign out. System forgets you're there, locks the doors again. Tomorrow you start back at
  the front door.

---

## The one-line story of the whole thing
> You **log in** → check your **desk** → **hunt** for healthcare workers → **chase** them with
> messages → pull the good ones into the **hiring line** → **grade & verify** them → **send them
> to the clinic** → keep the **clinic happy** → **track the money & stats** → **log out.**

Every fancy feature is just a helper hanging off one of those steps.
